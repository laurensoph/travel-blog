Skiing Spots

In mid winter outdoor temperatures can drop to -35 degrees celsius, so outdoor activities can be tricky. But there are many wonderful ski spots a short drive away - and if you're looking for a bargain many offer night skiing at discount prices.

Black Squirrel Books

Black Squirrel Books is a lovely bar/coffee shop/book shop where you can browse, have a prosecco or a cake all in one go!

American Football

The universities have their own football teams you can go watch play, or they have larger teams across Canada.
Transport

Transport in Ottawa varies from the local bus service to skating on the Rideau Canal in the winter. You can spot commuters, families and friends skating to where they need to be, across the city.

Food

Beavertail

A beavertail is a tasty flat doughnut type of desert. You can get an assortment of toppings, and you can buy them across the city and even on the canal in winter.

Poutine

A Canadian classic - frieds, cheese curds and gravy (with an variety of toppings!)

Cafes

Ottawa has a range of lovely coffees to hide away from the cold. Origin is a lovely place to work remotely as well.

Blog

What to do in a day

In the morning, head to the Canadian parliament. This is worth a visit directly then walking to the viewpoint by the national gallery. If it's warm, there's a lovely cafe with beautiful views. Have a walk around downtown, or if it's mid winter hire skates to get a tour of the city from a different angle. Stop for a pountine for lunch at any street vendor, and perhaps a beavertail for desert if you aren't too full. In the afternoon, Ottawa is host to many amazing galleries that are worth a visit. For example, the war or art museum.

National Gallery of Canada

What to do in 48h

Spend your first day wandering Ottawa's streets (or the canal). City hall has a fantastic free ice rink if you're a bit uneasy on your feet. Get well acquanted with downtown, and the viewspots. Enjoy a poutine for dinner, and visit a bar on Rideau.

On your second day, have a tour of a few of the extensive museums around the city. You can also book a tour of parliament and go up the viewpoint at the top. Enjoy a beavertail, and a coffee in one of the many cafes.

Ottawa Parliament Website

What to do in winter?

  • You can skate the full length of the canal if you have time, otherwise just popping along from one outdoor cafe to another is a worthwhile trip.
  • Hire a car (a truro is a super easy way to get around - like AirBnB but for cars) and drive to a nearby ski site. You can have a beginners lesson or hop straight on the slopes.
  • Visit an art or science gallery, it's a great way to get out of the cold.
