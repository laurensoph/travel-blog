Top Sights

Burj Khalifa

Visit the world's tallest building with more than 160 stories. Head to level 125 for amazing 360 views and an amazing skyline! Admission during non-prime hours cost 22£.

Dubai Mall

Visiting the Dubai Mall, which is the largest in the world will leave you with plenty of things to do. From exploring an aquarium, ice rink, decicious restaurants, and hundreds of stores to shop from.

Old Souk Market

Don’t forget to explore a traditional Arabian market. Also known as the Spice Souk, this is a must! Here you can find herbs, spices, salts, in their raw form, most popular, Saffron. In addition, you can learn a lot of the history and origins of spice. It is in Deira and adjacent to the Dubai Gold Sou and highly suggest to travel by metro or taxi. Get ready to bargain!

Desert Safari

A favorite amongst tourists, book a 6 hr Desert Safari excursion with your family and friends! Discover the beauty and customs of the Dubai desert, including a 4x4 bumpy ride right before sunset, a BBQ dinner and belly dancing show under the desert stars.

Palm Jumeirah

The palm tree shaped island is one of the coolest attractions in Dubai.

Louvre Abu Dhabi

If you love art then make sure to stop by the Louvre Abu Dhabi. Holding an impressive collection of more than 600 pieces,from ancient to contemporary art. Leonardo da Vinci and Pablo Picasso are some of the artists represented here and guaranteed that there is plenty to enjoy!

Sheikh Zayed Mosque

Located in Abu Dhabi, the Sheikh Zayed Mosque is the largest mosque in the UAE and a place of daily Islamic worship, holding up to 40,000 worshippers. You can really appreciate the astonishing architecture with Persian, Mughal, and Moorish influence.

Pro tip: Remember to dress modestly. Abyas are available free of charge at the mosque.

Ferrari World

An amusement park perfect for kids or car affectionados in Abu Dhabi!
Transport

Thankfully, Dubai and Abu Dhabi are cities where you do not necessarily need a car. The tram system in Dubai is state of the art and can get you up and down the city within an hour. There are multiple options to get to Abu Dhabi from Dubai and vice versa, which is only 170km away. The cheapest opition is a bus which takes approximately 2 hours and 20 minutes and costs about 7£. A taxi would only cut down the trip by 30 minutes and cost about 75£.

Food

Emirati Cuisine

Emirati food has undergone many changes over the eyars to reflect the culture and expats of the region. Dubai is an international hub, so you can find cuisines inspired from the rest of world. The two following dishes are native to Emirati culture.

Balaleet

Balaleet is a traditional Emirati sweet and salty breakfast dish. This breakfast dish, which is also sometimes served as a dessert, consists of vermicelli and eggs as the main ingredients, spiced with cardamom, cinnamon, saffron and orange blossoms!

Fattoush

Fattoush consists of fresh lettuce, diced tomatoes, cucumbers, mint leaves, onion, garlic, lemon and olive oil and is served on Levantine bread. You can find it almost anywhere and is also a great side option!

Culture

The majority of countries in the Middle East are Muslim and have strict rules that men andn women have to follow. Make sure to pack conservatively to show respect to local cultures and traditions. From my experiece, wearing a tank top and shorts is OK as longn as it's modest. Don't forget to bring a shawl or cover up just in case you enter a place where you need to cover up. Abu Dhabi is more conservative than Dubai so keep this in mind when traveling to this location.

More Information

What to do in 48h

Stay in Dubai and head early to the souk markets to explore the tradition and history. Then head to Dubai Mall for lunch and visit the Burj Khalifa which is right next to it. As the afternoon approaches, head to the beach for a remarkable sunset and dinner.

Abu Dhabi Day Tour

For day 2, book a full day tour with hotel pickup to Abu Dhabi from Dubai. This usually covers the Sheikh Zayed Mosque, Corniche Road, YAS Island, photos at Ferrari World, Heritage Village, and lunch!

What to do in a week

  • Day 1/2: Head to downtown Dubai for some attractions, waterfountains, a meal and then go to the Burj Khalifa. Enjoy the beach at the Dubai Marina.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Abu Dhabi to see the Sheikh Zayed Mosque. Make sure to stop at Ferrari World or the Louvre Abu Dhabi.
  • Day 4: Head to the Palm Jumeirah! Check out a cruise, or a kayak tour or paddleboard lesson. Then head to the Global Village for dinner and fun.
  • Day 5: Discover Dubai Parks such as IMG World, LEGOLAND Dubai.
  • Day 6/7:Spend the final two days returning to the beach to soak up the sun and visit the souks! On Day 6, make sure to book a desert safari adventure and on day 7, tour the souks around Dubai. Perfect way to end your trip!
