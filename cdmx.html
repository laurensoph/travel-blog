Mexico City

Sights Transport Food Blog
Top Sights

El Zocalo

It is the main square and heart of Mexico City, the capital. Prior to the colonial period, it was the main ceremonial center for the Aztecs. Today, it is home to the ‘Palacio Nacional (presidential palace)’ and government offices. It is also the central area for protests, cultural events, and concerts.

Tianguis

Head to the tianguis, which are open markets held on certain days during the week. You can find food, antiques, souvenirs, clothing, fabrics, you name it! Grab a beer and make sure you aren’t in a rush!

Teotihuacan

Teotihuacan, meaning “the place where the gods were created,” is located 50km northeast of Mexico City. It is an ancient Mesoamerican city designated as an UNESCO World Heritage Site. Here you can discover, the Avenue of the Dead and the Pyramid of the Moon, Pyramid of the Sun, the Temple of Quetzalcoatl as well artifacts of Teotihuacan culture.
Do not miss it!

Basilica of Our Lady of Guadalupe

Welcome to the Shrine of Our Lady of Guadalupe and home to "La Tilma", which depicts the miraculous 500 year old image of the Blessed Virgin Mary. As the world's most visited Catholic pilgrimage site, this is a must-see.

Frida Kahlo Museum

Go back in time to the historic and art museum dedicated to the life and work of Mexican artist Frida Kahlo. It is located in one of the oldest and most beautiful neighborhoods in CDMX, Colonia.

Palacio de Bellas Artes

Catch theatre, opera, literature, music, or dance at Mexico City’s cultural center or swing by to see the amazing exhibitions of painting, sculpture, and photography. Home to hosting some of the most notable events in the arts, the Palacio de Bellas Artes has been called the "Cathedral of Art in Mexico".

Bosque de Chapultepec

With more than 1,600 acres, the Bosque de Chapultec is a beautiful park to keep you entertained. The park boasts a zoo, multiple museums, a botanical garden, lake, monuments, and plenty of beautiful views for pictures. Perfect for an afternoon!

La Condesa

Is the fashionable, hip, charming neighborhood in CDMX with tree-lined streets and plenty of small restaurants, cafes, and boutiques. Highly recommend staying in this neighborhood, as its close to the city center, if not stop by for great food!
Transport

“Sitio taxis” which are official taxis registered to specific local or hotel and Ubers are the most inexpensive and safest way to travel within the city. Make sure to be vigilant and smart when traveling as you would in any other country.

Food

Tacos Al Pastor

Known for its spit-roasted, pineapple infused pork with a touche of pineapple. Yum! Order on the go or dine street side.

Esquites

Think dress corncobs without the cob. Kernels are removed and either boiled or roasted, then served with your choice of salt, lime, mayonnaise, chile powder or con todo (the works).

Quesadilla

Why order a quesadilla? Isn't it just with cheese? Not here! You must specify you want cheese, but try it without in its traditional native form and see how delicious it is!

Blog

Culture

The capital has an incredible mixture of ancient and modern complements that Mexico City truly unique. Pre-Hispanic ruins are still visible throughout the city, along with colonial Spanish, 19th-century Mexican, and modern buildings. Immerse yourself and get lost in Mexico City’s rich culture, history, and cuisine.

More Information

What to do in 48h

Start your day with some "huevo revueltos or chilaquiles y agua fresca" (scambeled eggs with refried beans and freshly squeezed juice). Head to Bosque de Chapultepec and from their hop in a taxi and head to El Zocalo. Walk over to el Palacio de Bellas Artes to see the murals of Diego Rivera followed by tacos and a beer in La Condesa.

Teotihuacan Day Tour

Avoid the crowds by booking an early morning tour of the Teotihuacan pyramids and a tequila tasting. Tour the sight, take pictures, and learn about Teotihuacan's historical influence. Cap your trip with a visit to obsidian factory where you can enjoy tastings of tequila, mezcal, and pulque!

What to do in a week

  • Day 1/2: Explore Mexico City and everything it has to offer! See el Zocalo, the Basilica of our Lady of Guadalupe, Palacio de Bellas Artes and more.
  • Day 3: Spend the day exploring el Bosque de Chapultepec!
  • Day 4: Find a tianguis for some shopping. End your day with a dinner at a Mexican Folkloric show.
  • Day 5: Head to Teotihuacan to see the pyramids.
  • Day 6/7: There are plenty of museums to check out once you tour Frida Kahlo's Blue House, which are filled with history and culture.
