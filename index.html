South Korea

Top Sights

Changdeokgung Palace

The grandest of the 5 palaces built by the Joseon dynasty built in the East of the city. For separate admission you can enter the Emporer's private gardens which is well worth the price of Â£5

Cheonggyecheon Stream

This ancient stream runs through the centre of Seoul. It is possible to walk along the stream for miles and, with trees lining it, it's sometimes hard to remember you're in the centre of one of the busiest cities in the world

Namsan Tower

The view from Namsan tower is gorgeous and offers 360 views of the entire city. It is best to walk up just before sunset so you can watch from the cafe with a bottle of beer as the city turns on their lights

Cafe's

South Korea is packed wth cafe's- an estimated 60,000- and is 'cafe culture' is now deeply embedded in their society. Ranging from Meerkat cafes, to Harry Potter cafes or posh fancy cafes, there's something for everyone

Dongdaemun Design Plaza

Based in the North of Seoul, the design plaza is the center for all things shopping. Surrounding the plaza are 24h shopping malls, complete with a jeweltry market operating from 9pm-5am

DMZ

The famous demilitarised zone that separates North Korea from South Korea is well-worth a visit and can be done with tour operators as a day trip from Seoul. You can use binoculars at various lookout points to see North Korea kids playing football

Heyri

This is a gorgeous town and known for it's creativity. It's bussling with art museums, libraries, bakeries and modern archiatecture. Located close to the DMZ, it's supposed to show North Korea how liberal the South is

Gamcheon Culture Village

Based in Busan, this culture village was revamped a few years ago and painted to attract more tourism and money to the area. It has windey streets leading to museums, art galleries and there are many coastline walks that end up in the village
Transport

Transport in South Korea is relatively simple. Most signs and announcements are read out in English as well as Korean and you can always find a lovely stranger to help you out if you get stuck. Most travel around the large cities is by the Metro which is very foreigner friendly and by far the cheapest and quickest way to travel. For trips further afield, the high-speed trains are amazing and will get you there faster than the airport, but they can be pricey. Buses can also be a good way of getting around as they are cheap. However, most annoucements are read out in Korean so it can be difficult to know when your stop is.

Food

Korean BBQ

This is one of the tastiest meals in Korea and can be found everywhere. You cook the pork belly at your table and eat it in a lettuce leaf with lots of other condoments.

Octopus

One of the Korean delicacies that is served is octopus. Very fresh and still still squirming on your plate- seasoned with some salt and pepper.

Korean Fried Chicken

Deep-fried and marinated in every sauce you can think of. This dish is mainly ordered on the banks of the Han River in Seoul and can be delivered direct.

Blog

What to do in a day

In the morning, head to Changdeokgung palace in Seoul, and remember to go via a hanbok shop to wear the traditional clothes and get free entry! Then head to Gwangjang market to try the local delicacies of octopus, or if you're feeling less adventurous then eat some seafood pancakes. Head out into the sun and eat your haul next to the Changdeokgung stream. Once you're finished, walk upstream to get to the Bukchon hanok village where the small traditional lanes offer an insight into traditional life and have views of Seoul. For dinner, catch the subway to the Han River and watch the sunset over the city whilst munching fried chicken and drinking beer.

What to do in 48h

Firstly head to the Han river and stay there for the morning. Whilst there, you can set out a camp, rent a bike to cycle along the river or even try your hand at watersports. Then for the afternoon, organise with a tour company a trip to the DMZ and have glimpse into North Korea.

DMZ tours

For the evening, go to Itaewon for a deicious BBQ at the Maple Tree (book ahead) and party until the subway reopens at 7am (or get a taxi back to the hotel). For the second day, follow the advice for the 24h's in Seoul.

What to do in a week

  • Day 1/2: Stay in Seoul and discover the Changdeokgung palace, the traditional foods and find some time to relax by the Han River.
  • Day 3: Take a trip to the DMZ/JSA to get a glimpse of life in North Korea. Be sure to book with a tour operator in advance.
  • Day 4: Hike up the Bukhansan mountain which is a gorgeorus walk through nature and gives the best views of Seoul. On the way down, stop off for icecream.
  • Day 5: Fly to Jeju Island from Gimpo Airport (20mins from central Seoul). Head down to the beach for a cocktail or glass of wine
  • Day 6/7:Spend the final two days in Jeju soaking up its natural beauty as a tropical island filled with waterfalls and beaches.
